# Mathematical Foundations for Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Overview
This appendix provides the essential mathematical foundations required to understand and implement humanoid robotics systems. These concepts are critical for understanding robot kinematics, dynamics, control, and perception.

## Linear Algebra
- Vectors and matrices for representing positions and transformations
- Rotation matrices and quaternions for orientation representation
- Eigenvalues and eigenvectors for system analysis

## Calculus
- Derivatives for velocity and acceleration calculations
- Integrals for motion planning and trajectory generation
- Partial derivatives for multi-variable systems

## Kinematics
- Forward kinematics: calculating end-effector position from joint angles
- Inverse kinematics: calculating joint angles from desired position
- Jacobian matrices for relating joint velocities to end-effector velocities

## Dynamics
- Newton-Euler equations for rigid body motion
- Lagrangian mechanics for complex multi-body systems
- Centroidal dynamics for humanoid balance

## Probability and Statistics
- Bayes' theorem for sensor fusion
- Gaussian distributions for uncertainty representation
- Kalman filtering for state estimation

## Control Theory
- PID controllers for basic system regulation
- State-space representation for system modeling
- Linear quadratic regulators for optimal control

## Optimization
- Convex optimization for trajectory planning
- Nonlinear optimization for complex control problems
- Model predictive control formulations

## Key Takeaways
- Mathematical foundations are essential for understanding robot behavior
- Different mathematical tools are appropriate for different robotics problems
- Practical implementation requires numerical methods and computational efficiency
